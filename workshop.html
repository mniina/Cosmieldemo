About This Workshop

Ikebana is the Japanese art of flower arranging — but it is much more than decoration. It is a practice of presence: of listening to each stem, honoring the space between branches, and finding meaning in what you leave out as much as what you put in.

This workshop is rooted in the teachings of 草月 Sogetsu Ikebana, one of Japan's most expressive schools. We'll explore the principles that give ikebana its depth — Ma (the meaning of space and interval), line, and the three structural elements of shin, soe, and hikae. Then you'll create your own moribana arrangement to take home.

No experience is needed. Just curiosity, and a willingness to slow down and be present with living things.

A pedestal and lightbox will be available for participants to photograph their finished arrangements — so you leave with both a polaroid memento and a beautiful image to keep or share.
